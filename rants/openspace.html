Openspaces

Every once in a while I stumble upon a person who doesn't loathe openspace offices or even asks what's wrong with them?

Let me fucking tell you what's wrong.

Disclaimer: This is a rant. Don't expect citations or hard scientific evidence everywhere. Although I'll keep adding links to be more annoying.

Definition

I'm using openspace as shorthand for open space office layout.
For me this means every office space, regardless of its size or number of occupants, that isn't an enclosed room.
Enclosed offices "housing" more than two workers share a number of problems with openspace, but I call them rotten compromise. They're a bit better.

It's made of people

It sickens me to my stomach to even have to outline all the problems.

You're in a room full of people for most of your waking hours.
You are there to perform mental labour, requiring your intelligence and focus, to benefit your employer and yourself.
People are sophisticated life forms. People use oxygen. People have their own smell. Their own unique microbiome.
People exhale warm gas full of carbon dioxide, water vapour, odours. People spit saliva out of their mouth every time they speak. Wild rave party for bacteria and viruses!
People generate heat and secrete sweat. They need to eat and excrete. They get sick. They are sad. They're happy. They're excited or tired.
Human speech consists of powerful sound waves in broad frequency range with insanely high amplitudes. People move almost all the time, it's unnatural to remain motionless for extended periods.
People react strongly to other person's behaviour, gestures, mood and especially speech. That's molded by ages of evolution.

I'm not saying people are disgusting. They present technological challenge when put together under a roof ;)

Ineffective

Openspace offices are ineffective in almost every way imaginable.

There is no privacy

I've seen people astonished that I event speak about privacy at WORK. I feel very sorry for them. Signing up for work is not an approval for intrusion!
In openspace you are constantly either being watched, or feel being watched. Unless you like them to watch, that's a stress factor.
There is no personal space. There is no comfort (more of that later). These are very important for your long term health, well-being and mental performance.
